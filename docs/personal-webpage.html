What I've built

The tool I use
every day

This is my personal command center — a purpose-built desktop application that replaces ad-hoc spreadsheets with a structured, auditable system for managing multi-framework AI governance engagements.

01
GRC Command Center
AI Governance · Risk · Compliance — Desktop Application
An AI governance professional managing multiple client engagements cannot rely on memory or scattered documents to track hundreds of specific legal requirements across overlapping regulatory frameworks. The EU AI Act alone contains 83+ distinct obligations that vary by risk tier and supply-chain role. Multiply that across ISO 42001, ISO 23894, NIST AI RMF, and the OECD AI Principles — then across multiple clients, each with different AI systems and risk profiles — and the cognitive load becomes unmanageable without tooling.

This application externalizes that cognitive load into a structured system. Every assessment decision, every status change, every piece of evidence is logged with an immutable audit trail. When a regulator or client asks "show me your diligence," the answer is the export from this tool.

GRC Command Center — full-stack Rust desktop app for multi-framework AI compliance
Designed, specified, and built a local-first desktop application that manages AI governance engagements across five regulatory frameworks. 241 seeded requirements from the EU AI Act, ISO 42001, ISO 23894, NIST AI RMF, and OECD AI Principles — with cross-framework reference mapping, per-system compliance assessment, risk matrix visualization, evidence vault, FRIA workflow, immutable audit trail, and LLM-powered regulatory assistant.
